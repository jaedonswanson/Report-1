\section {Statistical Methods}
\subsection {Software}

All figure creation and data analysis was completed in R 4.5.2. Packages used at well as specific code can be found in the attached appendix.

\subsection {Impact of Treatment on Blood Pressure}

The first analysis completed was to assess if treatment group had an impact on blood pressure (BP). This was completed by an analysis of variance (ANOVA) comparing BP reading to received treatment. After the ANOVA a Tukey-Kramer test was used to compare groups for differences.

\subsection{Impact of Age on Treatment Effectiveness}

The next analysis completed was in order to assess if a persons age had an impact on the effectiveness of the treatment they received. To do this linear models were first created for both an interaction as well as no interaction. These models were then compared using an analysis of covariance (ANCOVA) to examine if the interaction model predicted treatment effect significantly better than the non-interaction model.

\subsection {Impact of Sex on Treatment Effectiveness}

The last analysis completed was to examine if sex had an impact on treatment effectiveness. This was done using a two-way ANOVA to compare treatment effect between sex's. After the ANOVA was completed, a Tukey-Kramer test was again used to look at differences between specific groups.

\section {Results}
\subsection {Impact of Treatment on Blood Pressure}

The only treatment that was effective at significantly lowering blood pressure was Medication A. It was significantly better than both the placebo group ($P_{adj} < 0.001$, ) and medication B ($P_{adj} < 0.001$).

\subsection{Impact of Age on Treatment Effectiveness}

A persons age did not  have a significant effect on the effectiveness of a treatment ($P = 0.928$, $F_{d.f = 2} = 0.074$).


\subsection {Impact of Sex on Blood Pressure}

A persons sex did not have a significant impact on the effectiveness of a treatment ($P = 0.116$, $F_{d.f. = 2} = 1.815$). However, for all groups females had significantly lower BP than the males. In the placebo group the females had a significantly lower BP than males ($P_{adj} = 0.003$). Females receiving medication A also had significantly lower BP than the males ($P < .001$). Finally, women on Medication B again had a significantly lower BP than the males receiving the same treatment ($P = 0.003$).


\section {Conclusions}

If a person is advised to take medication in order to lower their blood pressure, they should take medication A. This is because it is significantly better than both no medication or medication B for persons of any age or sex.
